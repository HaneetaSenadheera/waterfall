16 Beautiful Waterfalls in Sri Lanka

Beautiful Waterfalls in Sri Lanka

With more than 30 waterfalls above the height of 10 m, Sri Lanka is a treasure trove for all these mesmerising cascades that it keeps safe in its nooks and corners. Here's our list of some of the best waterfalls in Sri Lanka:

Here is the list of 16 Beautiful Waterfalls in Sri Lanka

1. Diyaluma Falls

An hour away from Ella, Diyaluma Falls is the second-highest among the waterfalls in Sri Lanka. The waterfall tumbles down from a height of a whopping 220 meters, with several beautiful natural infinity pools at different levels along its length. Diyaluma Falls forms along the Poonagala Oya, a minor tributary of the river Kirindi Oya. The highlight of Diyaluma Falls is the hike to its top, swimming in the serene pools, and the magnificent views of the emerald hills.

2. Ramboda Falls

Ramboda Falls lies in the Pussellawa district in the city of Kandy. The area is known for its marvelous landscapes comprising highlands, ridges, plains, streams, and hilly mountains. There is a 20-minute hike to reach the top of this waterfall in Sri Lanka, but there are concrete steps and railings along the way. A parking facility is available at the base of the falls. Entry Fee: LKR 20

3. Bambarakanda Falls

Bambarakanda Falls, an hour away from the Horton Plains National Park, has the enviable distinction of being the tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka and one of the most spectacular in the monsoon. Bambarakanda Falls is at a height of 263 m and flows from the Walawe River. Enjoy the sight of the waterfall from the viewing platform, which is reachable by a small hiking trail. One can also visit a few smaller waterfalls in the vicinity, such as the Lanka Ella Falls.

4. St Clair's Falls

An idyllic representation of Sri Lanka’s natural being is the famous St. Clair’s Falls. Referred to as the Little Niagara of Sri Lanka, the waters of St. Clair run cold but are never weary of the gushing force. It is located in the Talawakele town of Nuwara Eliya District, and St. Clair’s stands as one of the widest falls in Sri Lanka. At an altitude of 1198 meters, St. Clair's Falls has an observation deck and a canteen at the entrance.
